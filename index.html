Born A Crime

Introduction

According to Wikipedia, Born a Crime: Stories from a South African childhood
is an autobiographical comedy book written by South African comedian Trevor
Noah, published in 2016. Trevor narrates stories from his childhood in a
humourous manner. The book intertwins the apartheid and his life. The title
Born a crime is supported to different parts of the book,for instance:
when he explain how the apartheid made a law that stated that black South
African should not intermarry with the white people. Noah was born a crime
for this parents went aganist this law.

Renaming Born A Crime

I would rename the Born a crime - "Echoes of My Childhood".
I would say the book narrates Trevor's childhood and most of what happened during his childhood has shaped him to who he is today. He also connects his
personal memories to the larger historical happenies of that time. For Trevor to Navigate through the apartheid he used humour as armour. He used it to talk
to people and fit in and even to get out of trouble. One of his career paths ends up being comedy.where he uses humour to make a living.

Reflective blog

"Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love with subservient women… 'He only wants a woman who is free because
his dream is to put her in a cage.'”

Abel like most men want to control their women, whereby you will find out that these women end up not having a say in their own marriages. This indeed
make these women caged and have no say in this marriages. There are cases where some women voice out and speak up about this sort of oppression
but not all.

Traditionally, most women were expected to be submissive to their husbands and serve them. I would say they were raised up to be kept by men. Unlikewise,
in morden relationships this is not the case. In modern society the definition of gender roles seems to have changed. What was termed to be men jobs
and roles, women roles and jobs has turned to be an outdated notion. Let's take an example of a career path like engineering, previously it was a field
dominanated by men, over the years this has changed and more women have taken the space and dominanated.

Just as Particia defied the rules of tradition and made a different life for herself, most women tend to do the same currently. They aspire to take roles
which previously we designated only for men, and even do them better than men. Though society still expects her to be under a man. Now women choose
who they want to become and go an extract mile of creating their own destinies and making their own stories.

My opinion would be: Love should not be a way for a man to cage a woman but it should be honouring each other's freedom, even when the tradition dictates
otherwise. True parternship is built on mutual respect, not outdated expectations. In emporing each other, bith men and women can achieve a more balanced
and fulfiling life together.
